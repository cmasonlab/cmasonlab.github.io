About the lab

Serious science.
Passionate inquiry.

The Mason Research Lab brings together critical social psychology, organizational behavior, and justice scholarship to produce rigorous, theory-grounded research.

We are committed to producing scholarship that does not just describe the world, but helps change it. Our work centers the experiences of marginalized communities and examines the psychological and structural roots of inequality.

Theoretical rigor
Our work is grounded in established social psychological theory, applied with precision to real-world problems.
Critical perspective
We examine power, bias, and oppression through a critical lens, asking not just what is, but what should be.
Empowerment through evidence
Research findings translate into tools, frameworks, and insights that empower communities and drive policy change.
What we study

Research areas

Unifying theme
Bias, discrimination, and the harms of oppression run through all of our work. We study how inequality operates psychologically and structurally, and what it takes to dismantle it.
01

Restorative justice

Studying public attitudes toward restorative justice policy and the social-psychological predictors of support for equitable approaches to harm and accountability.

02

Work-nonwork dynamics

Investigating the intersection of work and nonwork roles, group processes, and equity within organizational contexts.

03

AI, identity, and educational equity

Examining AI as both a social force and a practical tool, including how it shapes student learning, motivation, and self-efficacy; how it perpetuates or disrupts bias; and how it might advance equity in educational and organizational contexts.
