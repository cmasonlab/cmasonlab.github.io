We have a limited number of research assistantships available. Here is what you need to know.

Research assistantships

Why join?

The value in these highly sought-after opportunities is in the skills and experience you will gain. This may include professional presentations and publications (which are always our goal), as well as the opportunity to work closely with a faculty mentor who can advise you and write strong, detailed letters of recommendation on your behalf for graduate school and employment.

Although the position is unpaid, we can arrange for you to earn academic credit.

Completion of PSYC 220 (Research Methods & Data Analyis) is required. However, curiosity, conscientiousness, and a genuine interest in the work matter just as much.

What you will work on

Research assistants contribute to active projects in the lab, which currently span restorative justice, work-nonwork dynamics, and an emerging agenda on AI and social psychology. Projects may involve literature reviews, survey design, data collection, and analysis.

What we ask of you

A commitment of at least 3 hours per week for the duration of the academic year. We ask for reliability, intellectual engagement, and a collaborative spirit. You do not need to have all the answers; you need to be willing to learn.

How to apply

Send an email to Dr. Mason at cmason@marybaldwin.edu with the subject line "MRL RA Application." Please briefly describe:

  • Any relevant skills and prior experiences, and courses taken in Psychology so far
  • Why you are interested in research and in this lab in particular
  • What you hope to get out of the experience
