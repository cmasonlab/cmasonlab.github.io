Principal Investigator

Chandra D. Mason, Ph.D.

Dr. Chandra D. Mason is a social-personality psychologist whose research examines the harms of bias and discrimination on marginalized groups and public attitudes toward justice for disempowered populations.

She is Professor of Psychology and Department Chair of Psychological Sciences at Mary Baldwin University, where she directs the Mason Research Lab. Her methodological expertise includes psychometrics, structural equation modeling, path analysis, quasi-experimental methods, and longitudinal designs.

Dr. Mason holds a doctorate in Critical Social-Personality Psychology from the CUNY Graduate Center. She has served as an affiliated professor at the University of Virginia and as a consultant to organizations including the Walter Reed Army Institute of Research, Families and Work Institute, and Catalyst.
