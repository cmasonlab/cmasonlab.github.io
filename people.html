Principal Investigator
Chandra D. Mason, Ph.D.
Professor of Psychology · Department Chair
cmason@marybaldwin.edu LinkedIn
Download CV

Dr. Mason is a critical social-personality-organizational psychologist with more than 25 years of experience conducting research that examines the harms of bias and discrimination on marginalized groups and public attitudes toward justice for disempowered populations.

She is Professor of Psychology and Department Chair of Psychological Sciences at Mary Baldwin University, where she directs the Mason Research Lab. Her methodological expertise includes psychometrics, structural equation modeling, path analysis, quasi-experimental methods, and longitudinal designs.

Dr. Mason holds a doctorate in Critical Social-Personality Psychology from the CUNY Graduate School and University Center. She has served as an affiliated professor at the University of Virginia and as a consultant to organizations including the Walter Reed Army Institute of Research, Families and Work Institute, Catalyst, the Murphy Deming College of Health Sciences at MBU, and a Big Four professional services firm.

Elizabeth Jackson
Research Associate (2024-Present)
jacksone6975@marybaldwin.edu LinkedIn
Elizabeth joined the Mason Lab at Mary Baldwin in 2025. A Mary Baldwin alum, her background in psychology continues to inform her research interests, which include misinformation, coverage bias, media effects, and political communication.
Professionally, she serves as Program Administrator for the Center for Public Safety and Justice at the University of Virginia, where she supports research, training, and collaborative initiatives focused on public safety and criminal justice.
Outside of work and research, she is an avid reader, gamer, and outdoor enthusiast who enjoys stand-up paddleboarding and still hopes to be a Mythbuster when she grows up.
