Our Research

Research areas
Restorative justice
We study public attitudes toward restorative justice policy and the social-psychological predictors of support for equitable approaches to harm and accountability. Current projects examine the role of social dominance orientation, mindfulness, and psychological essentialism in shaping policy attitudes.
Work-nonwork dynamics
We investigate the intersection of work and nonwork roles, group processes, and equity within organizational contexts, examining how individuals navigate competing role demands.
AI, identity, and educational equity Emerging
We examine AI as both a social force and a practical tool, including how it shapes student learning, motivation, and self-efficacy; how it perpetuates or disrupts bias; and how it might advance equity in educational and organizational contexts.
