Our Research

Our research spans multiple areas, unified by a critical approach and a dedication to evidence-based social change.

Research areas
Restorative justice
We study public attitudes toward restorative justice policy and the social-psychological predictors of support for equitable approaches to harm and accountability. Current projects examine the role of social dominance orientation, mindfulness, empathic concern, and psychological essentialism in shaping policy attitudes.
Work-nonwork dynamics
We investigate the intersection of work and nonwork roles, in pursuit of lives well-lived and organizations that thrive. Ongoing work in this area explores the relationship between educational experiences and the self-efficacy beliefs people carry into their work and life roles.
AI and the Social World Emerging
Our emerging work examines how people at any stage, from students to working professionals, build skill, competency, and understanding through their use of AI, and how that use shapes self-efficacy and motivation to keep learning. More broadly, we are interested in the reciprocal relationship between AI and society, how these systems reshape human behavior and institutions even as human values and choices shape their design.
