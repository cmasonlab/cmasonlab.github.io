What we study

Research

Theory-driven inquiry into bias, justice, organizational behavior, and the emerging role of AI in shaping social outcomes.

Our approach

A unifying thread

Unifying theme
Bias, discrimination, and the harms of oppression run through all of our work. We study how inequality operates psychologically and structurally, and what it takes to dismantle it.

The Mason Research Lab takes a critical social-psychological approach to understanding and addressing inequality. Our research is grounded in established theory and employs rigorous quantitative methods, including psychometrics, structural equation modeling, and quasi-experimental designs.

We are committed to producing scholarship that does not just describe the world but helps change it. Below are our current and emerging areas of focus.

Research areas

Current work

01

Restorative justice

We study public attitudes toward restorative justice policy and the social-psychological predictors of support for equitable approaches to harm and accountability.

Current projects examine the role of social dominance orientation, mindfulness, and psychological essentialism in shaping policy attitudes.

02

Work-nonwork dynamics

We investigate the intersection of work and nonwork roles, group processes, and equity within organizational contexts.

Ongoing work examines how individuals navigate competing role demands and how organizations can better support equitable outcomes across diverse employee populations.

03

AI, identity, and educational equity

We examine AI as both a social force and a practical tool, including how it shapes student learning, motivation, and self-efficacy; how it perpetuates or disrupts bias; and how it might advance equity in educational and organizational contexts.

This emerging research agenda investigates AI's role in social psychology broadly, asking both how AI affects people and how it might be harnessed in the service of justice.
